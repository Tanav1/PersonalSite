Twitter Sentiment Classification

At Penn State for one of my Data Science classes we had a final project. For my project, I decided to analyze the sentiments of tweets which I scraped from the Twitter API. My goal was to gather information about people's opinions on climate change.

After gathering the data, and cleaning it (removing redundancies, extra spaces, etc.), and splitting it into a training and testing set, I was able to build models to give me analytics of the data I was working with. By doing this I got experience with different Python libraries like NumPY, GraphViz, Scikit-Learn, Pandas, and MatPlotLib. I also was able to get practical experience with the statiscal method of cross validation with different folds.

After analyzing features in each model I was able to determine analytics about people's thoughts on climte change, whether or not people believe in it, and the general opinion on climate change in general

I was able to determine that about 70% of the 500 tweets I scraped from the Twitter API believed in climate change, and how it is an important issue. I also was able to determine that about 30% of scraped tweets did not believe in climate change. I also was able to group prominent features together in a word cloud to determine which words were the most important/used in these tweets regarding climate change.
